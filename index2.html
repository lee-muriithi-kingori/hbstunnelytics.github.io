Lee Muriithi Kingori is a 21-year-old software entrepreneur from Nairobi, Kenya — simultaneously enrolled in nursing school while building a world-class technology company from scratch. His story isn't one of privilege or resources; it's one of relentless self-belief and raw ambition, coded one commit at a time.

Entirely self-taught, Lee mastered programming through sheer determination — using free resources, late nights, and an obsessive drive to understand how things work. He built HBS Tunnelytics to prove that exceptional software can come from anywhere — including Africa.

The Story
From First Line
to Full Company

Lee's journey didn't begin with a CS degree or a Silicon Valley mentor. It began with a question: "How does this work?" — and an internet connection.

Balancing nursing studies with software development demands extraordinary discipline. But Lee sees both paths as deeply connected — both nursing and engineering are about helping people, solving problems, and showing up when it matters most.

He founded HBS Tunnelytics to make one statement: that world-class software doesn't require a Western address or an Ivy League degree. It requires talent, dedication, and the courage to start.
